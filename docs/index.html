What is
Metamodular
Architecture?

Metamodular architecture is the practice of designing spatial systems that are inherently transformable — built not as fixed structures but as responsive frameworks that evolve with their occupants, site conditions, and functional demands over time.

Where conventional architecture defines a space once, metamodular architecture defines a range of possible spaces. It draws from three interconnected disciplines — kinetic, modular, and transformable architecture — synthesising their logics into a single unified approach that maximises both function and spatial usability.

Every SAXOD project is conceived within this framework. A module is never merely a room. It is a spatial instrument — tuned to its client, responsive to its site, and capable of adapting as operations change.

>/ KINETIC ARCHITECTURE

Buildings that move.

Kinetic architecture introduces controlled, mechanical, or sensor-driven movement into the building fabric itself — rotating facades, retractable roofs, pivoting walls, and responsive skins. Structures respond dynamically to light, wind, temperature, or occupant instruction, shifting their form to optimise comfort, energy performance, and spatial experience in real time.

In SAXOD projects, kinetic principles govern facade panels, operable shading systems, and movable structural thresholds — allowing a single module to present entirely different spatial characters across the course of a day or season.

>/ MODULAR ARCHITECTURE

Precision at every scale.

Modular architecture constructs space from discrete, standardised, and repeatable units — each engineered to exacting tolerances and designed to combine, stack, extend, or stand alone. The module is the fundamental unit of spatial thinking: self-sufficient, connectable, and deployable. It allows complex spatial programmes to be assembled with the clarity of a system rather than the ambiguity of bespoke construction.

SAXOD applies modular logic to every client commission — defining a minimal unit that satisfies the client's core operational need, then layering adjacency, growth, and connection protocols on top. The result is architecture that can be built fast, extended logically, and understood immediately.

>/ TRANSFORMABLE ARCHITECTURE

Space that adapts to need.

Transformable architecture designs for change from the outset — spaces reconfigure, compress, expand, or subdivide in response to shifting functional requirements. Unlike kinetic architecture (which moves in real time) or modular architecture (which grows through addition), transformable architecture encodes multiple spatial states into a single built form. One space. Many possible configurations.

SAXOD deploys transformable thinking at the interior scale — designing walls that fold, floors that rise, furniture that integrates structurally, and thresholds that open or close programme boundaries. For clients like DAATCHI and ONSOFA, this means a single module can serve as atelier, presentation space, or focused research environment depending on the hour.
